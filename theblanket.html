The Blanket

The folding of the blanket lying on the divan’s left armrest is peculiar, causing its only nuances of colours. When there is a pleat, shadows occur. There is an exception to the rule previously set out. A ghastly dot, probably a residue of some linen pants, or maybe an extremely small piece of cotton that escaped from the surroundings and settled in the polyester comfort, disturbs the monotony of the blanket and allows one to fully understand what it means to be mauve. The blanket is folded with a certain technique materialising a hole. The cavity lingers by the sofa’s side, like a mouth that wants to be fed, or an open dripping vagina that just received a great amount of pleasure. The hole is hidden but it invites the eyes. When you stare, it turns into a breathing animal.
