The Cheeks

There is a cherry teapot on the light clingwrap of skin. On the condition that the tea is brewed, tree roots and moss. Dried, presented. Steaming, waiting to be served on a platter. To a duchess. There are rose petals, a ghostly presence on the cheeks of the lady. Even when she is dead and decomposed, a perfume. Looking precisely, there are small anomalies in the skin. There, she did not form perfectly. The face of the lady is not only plastic from barbie dolls or eighteenth-century paintings, but also crystals of ruby encrusted on eighty-three spots. Some are faded beneath suspicion. Some anyone would see if only they looked. There is one on the nose.
