The Collarbones

Sometimes, within these spaces of skin, wrinkles arise from folding. The lady was folded in foetal, a long time ago, and these creases are the remnants of the act of unfolding, no ironing. They resemble palm reading lines, great destinies and a fortuneteller losing money. Two of them are stretched out on the neck. One under the collarbone gets lost beneath the dress. There is a wishing well, in between two globes at the base of the neck, built to catch tears running up, down. A place to put a pen, a thumb, with force.
