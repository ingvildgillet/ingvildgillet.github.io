The Left Arm

There are three beauty spots in total and the first two sit next to each other. They form the eyes of a small animal lost in the forest, surprised. The deformed mouth of the creature is a chalky scratch on the lady’s skin. A sacrifice or an accident. If the beast runs down the arm, it will take a year. It will have to take a turn on the right, so it can access the rest. It will witness a spoiled cut like an ink jet and a carmine leather notebook, a wrinkled face. All hopes were concentrated in that turning point, few escaped to the fingers, most stayed, keeping company to the elbow. The third beauty spot prevails where the skin is harsher, and the tone denser. It’s the beginning of the forearm, like a sign.
