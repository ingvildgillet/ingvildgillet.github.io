The Left Foot's Toes

A lady’s foot, thinking the body is not only a corpse but all sorts of animals. Worms. The foot is alone and green. It is falling down. Where blood weighs a kilo, blood weighs a truck. The ankle is sharp on one side and matches the night on the other. The foot of the lady, the little toes, the veins wrapping the foot like a birthday gift, not a party, a gift for the beloved children’s funeral. The end. The foot of the lady. The lady. The end. The white wall and the door that opened wide.
