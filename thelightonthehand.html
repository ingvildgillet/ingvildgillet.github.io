The Light on The Hand

What was forgotten, and what will always be, because it cannot be transposed in writing, is the light that surrounds the lady and the divan. It brings a glow like in a lurid clinical shower. The first finger dulled by that glare. The tip of the nail like silver keratin bleach holds itself a little bit too high, a little unnatural, stillness moving with every heartbeat. Strangers are envious. They hang like small penises, three dicks in the throat of a swan, limp, shameful. The fingers wish they would be sucked firm and stiff as the pinkie. Jealousy in between. But the swan is angelic.
