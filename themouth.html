The Mouth

Juicy cranberries. Withered bloody wedding napery. A frog. Small lines like freckles emulating to rejoin. A mouth. A lady’s mouth or a crank’s mouth. A whore’s mouth, not a whore’s mouth, a child’s mouth. Blood oranges that she just ate perhaps. The mouth of the lady does not move when she poses, stays like an image, frail, wise like an image, covering the teeth. A mouth like an open swollen wound, that was kept from healing. It cannot, it can never be closed. The mouth closed.
