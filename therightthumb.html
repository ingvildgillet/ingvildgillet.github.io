The Right Thumb

The elbow rests on the sofa. Outlines are amplified by the sofa’s exhalation. It bends and flattens, creating nooks. At least the divan is comfortable. This matters as the lady has been posing for hours. Suddenly, the right hand fingers overlap the neck of the lady. They could be razors if this were a horror movie. The fingers that once were small penises haven’t moved, they are more coherent, but not the thumb. He is nothing, he is dead, the thumb is nowhere. He is the largest of them, yet he is less easily loved. The outlier of the fingers’ reputation.
